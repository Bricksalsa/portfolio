Blocked Throat

2024

Blocked Throat, and Surveillance Time—installations that construct expectations only to subvert them. These works, and the research that frames them, use failure as a method: friction, delay, and misalignment become strategies to reveal the invisible mechanics behind everyday interactions. The document examines how frameworks— both physical and digital—govern per ception and participation. It asks: Who is watching whom? Who controls the frame? And what if that control slips? Writing and making evolved together throughout this project. The chatbot, embedded in the document, performs the very questions the research asks: it forgets, glitches, and reframes, turning the act of reading into an embodied experience of mediation. This work reflects my broader practice: constructing systems that seem intuitive, only to twist.
