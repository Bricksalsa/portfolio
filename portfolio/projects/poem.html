poeM

2023

poeM is a machine that writes poems based on what it sees. Using a security camera and artificial intelligence, the work generates short poems that vary in style, tone, and subject—completely on its own terms. The AI decides not only what to write, but when to write it, turning passive observation into active expression. Presented as a functional surveillance device, poeM creates unease by blending familiar systems of control with the unexpected presence of creativity. Developed as my final bachelor project, poeM explores authorship, agency, and the role of AI in shaping cultural production. It engages with societal questions about automation and creativity, while also experimenting with scenography and spatial storytelling to draw in the viewer. The physical prototype, accompanying signage, and printed poems were all designed to evoke the unsettling duality of a machine that watches and writes. This project marked a turning point in my practice—shifting from making visually pleasing installations to telling layered stories through technology. It helped define my identity as a designer and artist working with systems, perception, and the unexpected.Poe-m 1. Date: 2023 Dimensions: Variable Materials: IP camera, AI model, metal signage, printed poem output Thanks for Watching is an installation that stages and subverts the act of spectatorship. Visitors enter a dark, curtained space where they’re invited to pay — by coin or card — to briefly look through a small window. Behind the window appears a mirrored red room, lit theatrically, containing a surveillance camera and a screen. As the camera slowly turns to face the viewer, the screen reveals their own image, placing them unexpectedly at the center of the spectacle. What feels like a moment of private looking is quietly captured by the system: a photo is taken and used to generate a poetic AI-written description of the participant. This text is then shown outside the installation on a public LED ticker, turning each visitor into the subject of the next viewer’s curiosity. By weaving together payment systems, theatrical framing, surveillance tools, and generative AI, the work explores how looking is mediated, commodified, and redirected. Thanks for Watching turns the desire to see into a loop of exposure — where the viewer becomes content, the system becomes the storyteller, and no one remains outside the frame.2.Thanks for Watching Date: 2025 Materials: Coin mechanism, custom housing, theater lighting, curtains, AI-generated text, camera, Ticker Display NOMINATED FOR HKU AWARD This
